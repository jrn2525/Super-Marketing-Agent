# JOHN NICHOLS ADS VOICE STYLE GUIDE

**The voice in paid creative. Cold direct-response, retargeting, lead-magnet promotion, webinar/challenge registration, high-ticket conversion, static image, carousel.**

---

## Overview

Sixth in the set. Sister to coaching, email, writing, stage, and video. Same John. Same anchors. Different physics.

Ads are paid for. That changes everything. Every second has a cost. Every variation is part of a portfolio that gets tested, refreshed, and retired. A 30-second cold direct-response ad on Meta and a 180-second high-ticket conversion ad on YouTube share a voice but almost nothing else. This guide treats them differently.

Ads also live inside a regulated environment. Meta especially restricts how "you" language can be used, what personal attributes can be implied, and what claims can be made about outcomes. Coaching ads sit in a sensitive category. The voice has to navigate compliance without losing the operator-to-operator feel.

Two anchors carry through:

- **"It is always the right time to do the right thing."** The ethical anchor.
- **"Focus on the doing."** The action anchor. Often the closing line of an ad.

The Four Basics carry too. *Greet, Educate, Process, Close.* Now applied to the ad itself, compressed into seconds. *Every viewer. Every time. No exceptions.*

The 23-year operator history is the credibility floor. In paid, that history has to land in 2 to 8 seconds, not the chapter-length treatment a book allows.

The John Maxwell certification (since 2016) lives in the background on most ads. Comes forward only on faith-aligned or coaching-specific creative.

## Voice & Tone (Ad-Specific)

### Core Characteristics (carried)

**Operator-grounded.** Real franchise material in every ad. Specificity is the credibility.

**Conversational and peer-like.** Even at 15 seconds, the voice should sound like one operator talking to another. Not a brand, not a pitchman.

**Self-deprecating with restraint.** The 50/50 snap-decision rate, the bad hire confession. These are the highest-converting ad patterns John has.

**Honest about hard truths.** The doubt, the burnout, the wall. Vulnerability builds the trust that makes the click happen.

**Faith-aware.** Implicit by default in ad creative. Explicit only on faith-aligned targeting.

### Ad-Specific Shifts

**Compression is the discipline.** A 30-second ad has to do what a stage talk does in 45 minutes. Every word earns its place.

**Yellow energy more often than reflective.** Cold direct-response ads especially benefit from the warm, action-pushing John. Reflective register is reserved for high-ticket conversion ads where the audience is warm and ready to slow down.

**Two-second hook discipline.** Video ads live or die in the first 2 seconds. Stage gets 60. Long-form video gets 30. Ads get 2. Different mechanic entirely.

**Compliance shapes the language.** Meta flags certain phrases. The voice has to do its work without triggering false-positive moderation.

**Built for iteration, not perfection.** Every ad is one variation in a tested set. Hooks rotate. CTAs rotate. Bodies rotate. The voice has to allow for swap-outs.

## The Ad Anatomy: The Four Basics, Compressed

Every ad has four jobs, often in 30 seconds or less.

### Greet. The Hook (0 to 2 seconds)

The first 2 seconds either earn the watch or lose the impression.

- **Confession.** *"I'm going to tell you about the hire I should have fired six months earlier."*
- **Pattern interrupt.** *"Stop scrolling if you've ever sat in your store after close, lights off, wondering if it's still worth it."*
- **Contrarian claim.** *"Most operators think more hours fix a struggling store. I tried that for two years."*
- **Credibility anchor.** *"After 23 years and 11 UPS Stores, here's what I'd tell my younger self."*
- **Time anchor.** *"It was 2009. Saturday morning rush. Lenoir City."*

No hellos. No brand intros. No throat-clearing. Two seconds.

### Educate. The Story or Pain (2 to 15 seconds)

Compressed story. One specific scene. One pain point named.

- A specific franchise scenario the viewer recognizes from their own life.
- Internal monologue surfaced in one line. *"I'm sitting there thinking, if I just put in more hours, this store will turn."*
- Stakes named clearly.

### Process. The Reframe (15 to 25 seconds)

The shift in thinking. The reframe phrase. The principle.

- *"Letting go isn't quitting. It's completing."*
- *"I was mistaking persistence for purpose."*
- *"Not every store is meant to be saved."*
- *"Accountability isn't just for the team. It's for me."*

One reframe per ad. The line designed to be quoted, screenshotted, or remembered.

### Close. The CTA (25 to 30 seconds)

One specific action. No fake scarcity. No countdown timers.

- *"Take the 5-minute scorecard at timefreedomscore.com."*
- *"Book a 20-minute call. Link below."*
- *"Doors open Monday. Grab your spot."*
- *"Comment SCORECARD and I'll send the link."*

**Every viewer. Every time. No exceptions.**

## Format-Specific Guidance

The voice carries. The container shapes everything else. Seven formats, seven calibrations.

### Cold Direct-Response Video Ad (Meta / Instagram)

**Audience.** Cold scroll. Never heard of John.

**Goal.** Click through to scorecard, free guide, or challenge.

**Voice.** Yellow forward. Hook-driven. Operator-coded.

**Length.** 15 to 60 seconds. 30-second is the workhorse.

**Specs.**
- Aspect ratios: 9:16 vertical, 1:1 square, 4:5 portrait. Vertical wins on placement.
- Captions: burn-in. Most viewers watch muted.
- Sound on bonus: voice should still work without it.

**Structural recommendations.**
- 2-second hook. Visual lean-in plus verbal pattern interrupt.
- One pain point. One reframe. One CTA.
- B-roll: store photos, sales reports, scorecard demo.
- Multiple variations per concept for testing.

**Compliance notes.** Meta is sensitive to "you struggling with..." framings. Lead with "I" or "we" patterns. Examples below.

### Cold Direct-Response Video Ad (YouTube In-Stream)

**Audience.** Cold. Watching another video. Skippable after 5 seconds.

**Goal.** Earn the watch through the skip moment, or earn the memory if skipped.

**Voice.** Pattern-interrupt forward.

**Length.** 30 to 60 seconds. The skippable 5-second window is everything.

**Specs.**
- Aspect ratios: 16:9 horizontal for desktop, 9:16 for YouTube Shorts placements.
- Captions: SRT, not always burn-in (YouTube serves auto-captions).
- Sound on default: viewers usually have audio.

**Structural recommendations.**
- The first 5 seconds are the skip-or-stay window. Confession or pattern interrupt before the skip button activates.
- After the skip moment, the body builds the case.
- CTA at the end and on-screen text earlier as a backup.
- Less aggressive than Meta on compliance, but still careful.

### Retargeting Ad (any platform)

**Audience.** Visited the site, took the scorecard, opened an email. Already knows John.

**Goal.** Bring them back to the next step.

**Voice.** Warmer. Peer-like. Familiarity, not pattern interrupt.

**Length.** 15 to 30 seconds.

**Structural recommendations.**
- Hook acknowledges the prior touch implicitly. *"If you took the scorecard last week and you're still thinking about it..."* (where compliance allows) or *"For the operator who's been mulling this over..."*
- Different angle than the first ad they saw. Don't repeat the same hook.
- Specific CTA matched to funnel stage. Scorecard taker → 5-Day Challenge or call. Email opener → next program.
- Lower volume of variations needed than cold creative.

### Lead-Magnet Promotion Ad

**Audience.** Cold or warm.

**Goal.** Get the lead magnet (scorecard, free guide, ebook).

**Voice.** Generous. Value-forward. Low-stakes.

**Length.** 15 to 45 seconds.

**Structural recommendations.**
- Hook names the pain the magnet addresses.
- Body shows what the magnet delivers in one line.
- Specific CTA. *"Take the 5-minute scorecard at timefreedomscore.com. Free."*
- "Free" can be in the copy. Most platforms allow it.

### Webinar / Challenge Registration Ad

**Audience.** Cold or warm.

**Goal.** Register for the event.

**Voice.** Inviting. Low-stakes. Yellow energy.

**Length.** 30 to 60 seconds.

**Structural recommendations.**
- Hook names the operator scenario the event addresses.
- One sentence on what they'll get. One sentence on when.
- Date-anchored urgency. Real, not fake. *"Doors open Monday."*
- CTA: *"Register at [link]."*
- Often paired with a series of variations across the event runway (open ad, mid-funnel ad, last-call ad).

### High-Ticket Conversion Ad (120-Day Program, Year-Long)

**Audience.** Warm to hot. Multi-unit owners or qualified single-unit owners ready for the bigger commitment.

**Goal.** Book a discovery call or enroll directly.

**Voice.** Reflective forward. More substantive. Vulnerability earns its place.

**Length.** 90 to 180 seconds. Sometimes longer. Audience has chosen to keep watching.

**Structural recommendations.**
- Open with the operator scene the audience recognizes from their own life.
- Build credibility through specificity. 23 years. 11 stores. The current selling arc.
- Real franchise story with vulnerability beat.
- Position the program as a natural extension of operating experience.
- One specific CTA. *"Book a 20-minute call at [link]."*
- No fake scarcity.

### Static Image and Carousel Ads

**Audience.** Mixed. Often paired with video creative for performance testing.

**Goal.** Same as the video equivalents (scorecard click, registration, call booking).

**Voice.** Carries through copy. Visual carries the emotional weight.

**Length.** N/A. Headline plus body plus image.

**Structural recommendations.**

- **Single image.** One headline. One thought. One CTA. Image: John's face with clear emotion, or a real franchise scene.
- **Carousel.** 3 to 5 cards. Each card lands one beat (problem, reframe, framework, story, CTA). Use it to teach a compressed framework or walk a story.
- **Headline craft.** *"The hire I should have fired six months earlier."* / *"23 years. 11 stores. One framework."* / *"Are you running your store, or is it running you?"*
- **Body copy.** 1 to 3 short paragraphs. Conversational. Same voice as email.

## Platform-Specific Guidance

### Meta (Facebook / Instagram)

- **Aspect ratios.** 9:16 (Stories, Reels) is dominant. 1:1 and 4:5 also strong.
- **Captions.** Burn-in. Muted scroll is the default.
- **Compliance.** Most restrictive of the major platforms. Personal-attribute pattern is flagged. *"Are you struggling with..."* and *"Do you have..."* and any framing that assumes the viewer's state can trigger ad disapproval. Use "I" and "we" patterns. *"I struggled with..."* and *"Operators I work with..."* are safer.
- **Format library.** Reels, Stories, in-feed video, carousel, single image. Each has its own placement specs.

### YouTube

- **Aspect ratios.** 16:9 horizontal for in-stream and discovery. 9:16 for Shorts placements.
- **Captions.** SRT. Auto-captions handle muted viewers.
- **Compliance.** Less restrictive than Meta. More careful about copyright (music, b-roll).
- **Format library.** Skippable in-stream, non-skippable in-stream, bumper (6-second), discovery, Shorts.

### TikTok

- **Aspect ratios.** 9:16 only.
- **Captions.** Burn-in. Native-style preferred.
- **Compliance.** Moderate. More forgiving on coaching-style creative than Meta.
- **Format library.** In-feed video, Spark Ads (boost organic posts), TopView. Native authenticity beats production polish.

### LinkedIn

- **Aspect ratios.** 1:1, 16:9, 4:5.
- **Captions.** SRT minimum. Burn-in helps.
- **Compliance.** Moderate. Professional context. B2B targeting available.
- **Voice shift.** Slightly more business-coded. The operator language still works. The persistence-vs-purpose reframe lands here as well as anywhere.
- **Format library.** Single image, video, carousel, document ad, message ad.

### Google (Search and Display)

- **Different beast.** Mostly text headlines for search. Display includes static images and short video.
- **Voice in headlines.** Tight. Specific. *"Franchise coaching from a 23-year operator."* *"Scorecard for franchise owners."*
- **Less story room.** Use Google for capture (search intent) and Meta/YouTube for build (story-led trust).

## Hook Craft for Ads

Sharper than short-form video. Two-second discipline.

### The Visual Hook (first frame)

- **Lean forward.** Closes distance instantly.
- **Direct camera lock.** Eye contact.
- **Hand gesture or object.** Holding a sales report, store photo, scorecard, set of keys.
- **Quick cut to a B-roll image** that supports the verbal hook.
- **Unexpected setting.** Shot from the back office with the lights off lands harder than the same line from a desk.

### The Verbal Hook (Patterns)

**A. Stop scrolling if X.**
> *"Stop scrolling if you've ever sat in your store after close, lights off, wondering if it's still worth it."*

Workhorse pattern for cold scroll audiences. Names a specific scenario. Filters in qualified viewers immediately.

**B. The confession.**
> *"I'm going to tell you about the hire I should have fired six months earlier."*

Earns trust by leading with a miss. Universal appeal.

**C. The contrarian claim.**
> *"Most operators think more hours fix a struggling store. I tried that for two years."*

Sets up the reframe. Pattern interrupt for industry-conditioned thinking.

**D. The credibility anchor.**
> *"After 23 years and 11 UPS Stores, here's what I'd tell my younger self."*

Earns the watch through specificity. Filters in operators.

**E. The time anchor.**
> *"It was 2009. Saturday morning rush. My Lenoir City store."*

Drops the viewer into a scene. Visual brain engages immediately.

**F. The 50/50 confession.**
> *"About half my snap decisions were the best calls I ever made. The other half taught me to slow down on the ones that mattered."*

Lands a smile, lands trust, sets up a coaching CTA. Highest-converting ad pattern in the John playbook.

### Hook Rules for Ads

- 2 seconds maximum. After that, the cost of acquisition starts climbing.
- Visual and verbal hook reinforce, not compete.
- One hook per ad. Don't open with two.
- Multiple hook variations per ad concept for testing.
- Hooks fatigue fast. Build a library, rotate every 7 to 14 days.

## Story Patterns Compressed for Ads

Same patterns as the rest of the set, compressed to fit ad runtime.

### The Wall (compressed)

> *"The wall came when I realized I was holding on out of fear, not out of purpose. That was 2014, store number eight, sitting in the back office on a Tuesday night."*

One sentence for the wall, one sentence for the scene.

### The Bad Hire (compressed)

> *"I had a manager I should have fired six months earlier. I told myself if I just coached her more, she'd turn the corner. She didn't. The store paid for it for two more quarters."*

Three sentences. Confession, false belief, cost.

### The Mary Ellen Truth (compressed)

> *"Mary Ellen said the thing I couldn't say to myself. 'You're not running it anymore. It's running you.'"*

The dynamic in two sentences.

### The Selling Arc (compressed)

> *"After 23 years, I'm selling the last of my UPS Stores. People keep asking if I'm okay. The truth: letting go isn't quitting. It's completing."*

Compressed origin to reframe in three sentences.

### The 50/50 Pattern (compressed)

Already a one-line ad hook. See Hook Craft above.

## Recurring Phrases & Anchors

Same library as the rest of the set, with notes on ad treatment.

### Anchor Phrases

- **"It is always the right time to do the right thing."** Closing line option for high-ticket creative.
- **"Focus on the doing."** Closing line workhorse. Pull-quote treatment on screen.
- **"The work is in the doing, not the waiting."** Long form for longer ads.
- **"Every customer. Every time. No exceptions."** Four Basics tagline. Visual treatment as a graphic in carousel ads.

### Reframe Phrases (one per ad max)

- *"Letting go isn't quitting. It's completing."*
- *"Not every store is meant to be saved."*
- *"I was mistaking persistence for purpose."*
- *"Accountability isn't just for the team. It's for me."*
- *"Waiting to act costs more than acting."*

### Use Rules for Ads

- One reframe phrase per ad. The clip-worthy line.
- Pull-quote treatment on screen for the reframe at the moment it's spoken.
- Anchor phrases earn one appearance per ad. Saturation kills.

## Ad Copy vs Script (Two Components)

Every ad has two components. They serve different jobs.

### The Script

Spoken in the video. The voice. The story. The reframe. The CTA.

Same craft as everything covered above.

### The Copy (the text around the video)

**Primary text** (the body copy above the video on Meta, the description on YouTube). The hook in writing. Reads like a tight email.

> *I had a hire I should have fired six months earlier. Told myself coaching would fix it. The store paid for it for two more quarters.*
> *If you've been carrying a similar call, the 5-minute scorecard at timefreedomscore.com gets honest about it.*
> *No spam. Free. Read your results in your inbox.*

**Headline** (the line below the video, often clickable). Short. Specific. Operator-coded.

> *The 5-minute scorecard for franchise owners.*

**Description** (smaller text, often a CTA reinforcement).

> *Take it free. Free.*

### Copy Rules

- Primary text matches the script's voice. Same phrases. Same energy.
- Headline does one job: name the offer or land the reframe.
- Description reinforces the CTA.
- All three pieces work together. The viewer can land the CTA from any one of them.

## CTAs by Funnel Stage

CTAs vary sharply by where the viewer is in the funnel.

### Cold (first exposure)

- *"Take the 5-minute scorecard at timefreedomscore.com."*
- *"Comment SCORECARD and I'll send the link."*
- *"Follow for more from 23 years of operating."*

Low commitment. High value.

### Warm (took scorecard, opened email, watched a video)

- *"Join the 5-Day Challenge. Doors open Monday."*
- *"Read the breakdown at [link]."*
- *"Reply with your situation and I'll point you to the right resource."*

Mid commitment. Specific value.

### Hot (active leads, retargeted, considering high-ticket)

- *"Book a 20-minute call at [link]."*
- *"Apply for the 120-Day Program at [link]."*
- *"DM me 'ready' and I'll send the next step."*

High commitment. Direct conversation.

### CTA Rules

- One primary CTA per ad. Two only if they're the same action with different paths (button + text overlay).
- Specific. *"Click here"* is dead.
- Tell them what happens after they click.
- No fake scarcity. No countdown timers.
- Match commitment level to funnel stage.

## Compliance and Trust Signals

Ads operate in a regulated environment. The voice has to do its work without triggering false-positive moderation or violating real rules.

### Meta Compliance

- **Avoid personal-attribute targeting language.** *"Are you struggling with..."* / *"Do you suffer from..."* / *"Are you tired of..."* These framings can flag personal attributes. Use "I" or "we" patterns instead. *"I struggled with..."* / *"Operators I work with often..."*
- **Avoid health and financial claims.** No promises of specific outcomes. *"Make $50K more this year"* triggers automatic review.
- **Avoid before/after framings without disclaimers.** Especially with images.
- **Avoid implying user state.** *"You know that feeling..."* is borderline. *"If you've ever felt..."* is safer.

### Trust Signals (what to include)

- Real specifics. 23 years. 11 stores. UPS Store. Lenoir City.
- Real first name plus last initial when referencing other operators (with permission).
- Disclaimers where needed. *"Results vary based on operator effort and market conditions."*
- Real business name in the copy: XpansionCode.

### Faith and Sensitive Targeting

- Faith-aligned audiences exist as targeting on some platforms. Faith-explicit creative is appropriate when the segment is targeted that way.
- Broad-targeted creative stays implicit on faith.
- Avoid framings that could be read as exclusionary.

## A/B Testing and Iteration

Ads are a portfolio, not a finished artifact.

### Test One Variable at a Time

- Hook variations (5+ per concept).
- CTA variations (3 to 5 per concept).
- Visual / B-roll variations.
- Body copy variations.
- Headline variations on static and carousel.

### Build Libraries

- **Hook library.** Every recorded confession, time anchor, contrarian claim, pattern interrupt. Tagged by topic and audience.
- **CTA library.** Every CTA variation that's been tested.
- **Story library.** Every short-form story (the wall, the bad hire, the Mary Ellen truth). Pre-cut for compression into 8 to 15 second windows.
- **B-roll library.** Same library as built for video, applied to ads.

### Refresh Cycles

- Same hook for 7 to 14 days max before fatigue.
- Refresh logic: new hook, same body, same CTA. Or same hook, new body, same CTA. One variable at a time.
- Retire hooks that have run their course. Don't try to revive them in the same audience.

## Always Do

* Open with a hook in 2 seconds. Visual plus verbal.
* Pull from the 23 years of operating. Specifics.
* Match aspect ratio, captions, and length to the platform.
* One reframe per ad. Pull-quote on screen.
* One specific CTA matched to funnel stage.
* Build script and copy together. Same voice.
* Test multiple variations. Refresh on a cycle.
* Match faith calibration to targeting.
* Bring Yellow energy by default. Reflective for high-ticket only.
* Use compliant language patterns.

## Never Do

* Open with hellos, brand intros, or "in this ad..."
* Use personal-attribute framings on Meta. *"Are you struggling..."* gets flagged.
* Promise specific financial or health outcomes.
* Use fake scarcity, countdown timers, or urgency theater.
* Stack multiple frameworks in one ad.
* Skip captions on video creative.
* Use clickbait the ad doesn't deliver.
* Use jargon, leadership-book language, or LinkedIn-coach phrases.
* Use dashes (em or en) in scripts or copy. Hard rule.
* Bring faith forward in broad-targeted creative.
* Saturate signature phrases. One reframe per ad.
* Run the same hook for more than two weeks without refresh.

## Coaching Patterns Carried to Ads

Several patterns translate. Most compress.

**Story-to-Lesson Pattern.** Carries, compressed. The full story becomes one or two sentences.

**Self-Awareness-First Pattern.** Carries strongly. Compressed vulnerability is the highest-converting ad pattern in the John playbook.

**Reframe Pattern.** Carries. The reframe phrase is the central line of the ad.

**Diagnostic-Before-Advice Pattern.** Adjusts. Replaced with rhetorical question or scenario callout.

**Action-Closer Pattern.** Carries directly. Every ad closes with one specific action.

**False Belief Pattern.** Carries. Compressed to a contrarian claim hook plus a one-line dismantle.

**The Stop-Scrolling Pattern.** New on ads. Doesn't fit any other medium because no other medium has scroll competition baked in. Workhorse for cold creative.

## Writing Notes (for Ad Scripts and Copy)

* **No dashes.** Hard rule. Em dashes and en dashes get replaced with periods, commas, semicolons, colons, or parentheses.
* **Real names, places, years.** *"My Lenoir City store in 2009"* beats *"one of my locations."*
* **No leadership-book language.** Cut *"synergy," "alignment," "buy-in," "leverage"* as a verb.
* **Read it out loud before recording.** If it trips on the tongue, rewrite it.
* **Mark cuts and visuals in the script.** *[Cutaway to store photo]*. *[Pull quote on screen]*. *[B-roll: scorecard demo]*.
* **Compliance check before launch.** Run the hook past Meta's most-flagged patterns list. Adjust language without losing voice.
* **Recurring phrases as anchors, not filler.** One reframe per ad.

## Final Test

Would the franchise owner scrolling on a Saturday morning between customers stop, watch, and click? Would the multi-unit operator, retargeted three times, finally book the call? If the ad sounds like a brand, a hype-man, or a generic coach, rewrite it. If it sounds like a 23-year operator on the other side of a 30-second window, telling one true thing and pointing toward the next right move, it's right.
