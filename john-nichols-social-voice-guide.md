# JOHN NICHOLS SOCIAL VOICE STYLE GUIDE

**The voice in social. Operator to operator. LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, X, YouTube Community, Threads.**

---

## Overview

Seventh and final in the set. Sister to coaching, email, writing, stage, video, and ads. Same John. Same anchors. Public-facing, multi-platform, conversational.

The framing for everything in this guide is **operator to operator**. Not a coach talking down to franchisees. Not a guru on a mountain. A peer who has run stores for 23 years and now also coaches, sharing what he's learned with people he respects. That stance shapes every post type, every platform, every CTA.

Social isn't one thing. It's at least seven platforms, each with different physics, different audiences, different algorithm logic, different content formats. A long-form LinkedIn post and a 30-second TikTok share a voice but almost nothing else. This guide treats them differently.

Two anchors carry through:

- **"It is always the right time to do the right thing."** The ethical anchor.
- **"Focus on the doing."** The action anchor. Often the closing line on operator-coded posts.

The Four Basics carry too. *Greet, Educate, Process, Close.* Now applied to the post itself. *Every post. Every time. No exceptions.*

The 23-year operator history is the credibility floor. UPS Store, Lenoir City, March 2003. Eleven stores at peak. Selling the last 10 now. That arc is the most-shared, most-saved, most-commented-on content in the playbook because the audience can feel its truth.

The John Maxwell certification (since 2016) lives in the background on most platforms, surfaces openly on faith-aligned content and coaching-specific posts.

## Voice & Tone (Social-Specific)

### Core Characteristics (carried)

**Operator-grounded.** Real franchise material in every post. Specifics over generalities.

**Conversational and peer-like.** Even in a 1,300-character LinkedIn post, the voice should sound like one operator talking to another at the coffee shop. Not a brand. Not a content creator.

**Reflective and honest.** Vulnerability is the trust engine.

**Self-deprecating with restraint.** The 50/50 snap-decision rate, the bad hire, the persistence-vs-purpose confession. These are the highest-engagement posts in the playbook.

**Values-driven.** Integrity, accountability, finishing well as throughlines.

**Faith-aware.** Implicit by default. Explicit on faith-tagged posts and platforms where the audience has signaled openness.

### Social-Specific Shifts

**Two energies, both real.** The reflective John (back-office confessions, Mary Ellen mirrors, completion stories) and the Yellow Quick Start John (quick observations, playful celebrations, action-pushing posts) are both authentic. A social mix that's only one register burns out the audience and burns out John. Both are needed.

**Public, not broadcast.** Social is a conversation. The comments are half the value. Replies, engagement on other operators' posts, lifting up community wins. A guide that treats social as publishing only gets it wrong.

**Hook discipline scales by platform.** LinkedIn lets the first three lines do the work. TikTok needs 1.5 seconds. Instagram Reels need a hook plus visual. X needs to land in 280 characters. Different platforms, different first-second mechanics.

**Saved and shared, not just liked.** The best posts get saved (bookmarked) and shared (sent to a peer or partner). That's the metric that matters for a coaching business. Saves and shares come from posts that crystallize a truth the reader wants to remember.

**Built for variety, not for one archetype.** A coaching feed that's only "back office confession" posts gets exhausting. A real social mix needs reflective posts, quick hits, questions, behind-the-scenes, recommendations, milestones, community celebration. The full register.

## The Social Anatomy: The Four Basics, Applied

Every post has four jobs.

### Greet. The Hook (the first line)

The first line earns the next line. On every platform.

- **LinkedIn and Facebook.** First three lines visible before the "see more" cut. Land the hook there.
- **Instagram feed.** First line of the caption truncates after a few words. Hook in the first line.
- **Instagram Reels and TikTok.** First 1.5 seconds. Visual plus verbal.
- **X.** The whole post is the hook unless it's a thread.
- **YouTube Community.** The first line decides the click.

### Educate. The Story or Insight

Compressed for the platform. A LinkedIn post can develop a story over 800 words. A TikTok lands one beat in 30 seconds. The same story, different runway.

### Process. The Reframe or Principle

The line that crystallizes the post. Designed to be highlighted, screenshotted, quoted, saved.

- *"Letting go isn't quitting. It's completing."*
- *"I was mistaking persistence for purpose."*
- *"The work is in the doing, not the waiting."*

One reframe per post. Make it the line.

### Close. The Reflection or Soft CTA

Social CTAs are softer than ads or email. Most posts close with a reflection, a question, or a soft direct.

- *"Are you running your store, or is it running you?"*
- *"What's the call you've been avoiding?"*
- *"Take the 5-minute scorecard at timefreedomscore.com."*
- *"Drop a comment with your year-one lesson."*

**Every post. Every time. No exceptions.**

## Platform-Specific Guidance

The voice carries. The container shapes everything else.

### LinkedIn

**Audience.** Operators, franchisees, business owners, coaches, B2B network.

**Goal.** Authority, pipeline, peer recognition.

**Voice.** Slightly more business-coded. Still operator-grounded. The reflective register lands hardest here.

**Format library.**
- Long-form text post (up to 3,000 characters, ~800 words)
- Carousel (PDF document, 8 to 10 slides typical)
- Native video
- LinkedIn article (long-form)
- Poll
- Image post

**Structural recommendations.**
- Hook in first three lines. Earn the "see more" click.
- Short paragraphs (1 to 3 sentences). Lots of white space.
- Reframe phrase as a standalone line, often with line breaks before and after.
- Soft close. Reflection or question.
- 3 to 5 hashtags, broad professional (#franchise #leadership #operations #franchisee).
- Cadence: 3 to 5 posts per week.

### Facebook

**Audience.** Mixed. Older operator demographic. Family connections. Franchise owner groups.

**Goal.** Community, relationship, top-of-mind.

**Voice.** Warm. Operator community-coded. Family-friendly. Faith can surface more openly on this platform than on LinkedIn or X.

**Format library.**
- Long-form text post (no hard limit)
- Photo post
- Native video
- Live stream
- Story (24-hour ephemeral)
- Group post (franchise owner groups)

**Structural recommendations.**
- Longer captions accepted. Up to 1,000 words still readable.
- Photo posts with personal scenes (store memorabilia, current life, the stores being sold) build connection.
- Group engagement matters as much as feed engagement.
- Cadence: 2 to 3 posts per week to feed. Active in groups.

### Instagram

**Audience.** Visual-first. Mixed demographic with younger skew on Reels. Discovery-driven.

**Goal.** Discovery, follower growth, visual brand-building.

**Voice.** Yellow more present. Visual energy matters. Less long-form.

**Format library.**
- Reels (9:16 vertical, 30 to 90 sec)
- Feed posts (square or 4:5 portrait, photo or carousel)
- Stories (24-hour ephemeral)
- Lives

**Structural recommendations.**
- Reels are primary discovery vehicle. Treat as short-form vertical (see video voice guide).
- Carousels for educational content. 8 to 10 slides.
- First line of caption truncates. Hook there.
- Save-worthy content beats like-worthy content.
- 10 to 15 hashtags, mix of broad and niche.
- Cadence: 4 to 7 posts per week across formats.

### TikTok

**Audience.** Cold algorithm distribution. Younger skew but business-content audience growing.

**Goal.** Discovery, follower growth, short-form clip distribution.

**Voice.** Yellow forward. Tightest. Hook-driven.

**Format library.**
- 9:16 vertical only
- 30 to 90 seconds typical
- Native authenticity beats production polish
- Captions burn-in essential

**Structural recommendations.**
- 1.5-second hook discipline.
- One beat, one payoff.
- Often clips from longer talks or videos repurposed.
- 3 to 5 hashtags, trend-aware.
- Cadence: 1 to 2 per day if pursuing growth, 3 to 4 per week as a maintenance rhythm.

### X (Twitter)

**Audience.** Real-time. Operators, writers, business commentators.

**Goal.** Top-of-mind, quick observations, thread distribution.

**Voice.** Tight. Direct. Less story room.

**Format library.**
- Single post (280 characters)
- Thread (multiple connected posts)
- Image and video
- Quote post

**Structural recommendations.**
- Threads work for the longer reflective posts. Each post in the thread is one beat.
- Single posts work for quick observations and the quote-post format.
- 1 to 2 hashtags max. Often zero.
- Cadence: 1 to 3 per day for active presence.

### YouTube Community Tab

**Audience.** YouTube subscribers.

**Goal.** Connection between videos. Community feeling.

**Voice.** Conversational. Behind-the-scenes friendly.

**Format library.**
- Text post
- Image post
- Poll
- Video clip teaser

**Structural recommendations.**
- Underused asset for most channels.
- Polls drive engagement and inform future content.
- Behind-the-scenes glimpses (selling the stores, current writing project, family events) build subscriber relationship.
- Cadence: 1 to 2 per week.

### Threads

**Audience.** Conversational, often crossposted from Instagram.

**Goal.** Conversation, top-of-mind for Instagram followers.

**Voice.** Looser than X. More conversational.

**Format library.**
- Text post (500 characters)
- Image and video
- Reply chains

**Structural recommendations.**
- Lower-stakes than Instagram or LinkedIn. Casual register.
- Reply to others as much as posting original.
- Cadence: 3 to 5 per week.

## Post Archetypes

Fourteen archetypes covering the real range of operator-to-operator content.

### 1. The Back Office Confession

**Hook.** Sets the scene. *"I'm sitting in the back office. Lights off. Just the glow of the computer screen."*

**Story.** A specific moment of doubt, burnout, or hard decision with sensory detail.

**Reflection.** Names the false belief and how he broke through it.

**Close.** A question or truth for other operators. No hard CTA.

**Best on.** LinkedIn, Facebook, long-form Instagram.

### 2. The False Belief Destroyed (Carousel or Post)

**Hook.** Names a myth common in franchising. *"Most operators think persistence is the answer. I was one of them."*

**Body.** Each slide or paragraph tackles a different angle. Persistence vs. purpose. Snap decisions. Letting go.

**Close.** A reflection prompt. Soft CTA optional.

**Best on.** LinkedIn carousel, Instagram carousel.

### 3. The Decision Autopsy

**Hook.** *"About half my snap decisions were the best calls I ever made. The other half taught me to slow down on the ones that mattered."*

**Story.** Walks through a real decision, the context, the outcome.

**Lesson.** The nuanced takeaway. Not all fast is good. Not all slow is good.

**Best on.** LinkedIn, Facebook, X (as thread).

### 4. The Mary Ellen Mirror

**Hook.** References a blunt truth from Mary Ellen.

**Story.** Describes the conversation and the clarity it brought.

**Reflection.** How it changed his approach.

**Best on.** LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram caption.

### 5. The Completion Not Quitting Post

**Hook.** *"After 23 years, I'm selling the last of my UPS Stores."*

**Story.** Emotional arc of letting go.

**Reflection.** Reframing as completion.

**Best on.** Every platform. This is the central narrative arc of the current season.

### 6. The Quick Observation

**Hook AND post.** A single sentence or two that lands a truth from operating life.

> *"You can't out-coach a hire who shouldn't be there."*
> *"The store that drains you is also the store that's teaching you."*
> *"Half the work of franchise ownership is deciding what not to do."*

**Best on.** X, Threads, LinkedIn, Instagram caption with image.

### 7. The Question Post

**Hook.** A direct question to the audience.

> *"What's the hardest hire you ever had to make?"*
> *"What's a belief about franchising you've had to let go of?"*
> *"What's running your business right now: you, or your business?"*

**Body.** Optional context (1 to 3 sentences).

**Close.** *"Drop a comment. I read every one."*

**Best on.** LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube Community.

### 8. Industry Commentary

**Hook.** Responds to franchise news, market shifts, or industry trends.

**Body.** Operator-grounded take. What it means for someone running a store.

**Reflection.** Personal experience that informs the take.

**Best on.** LinkedIn primarily. X for quick takes.

### 9. Behind-the-Scenes

**Hook.** Glimpse of current reality.

> *"Walked through store number nine for the last time today. New owner takes over Friday."*
> *"Three boxes of files from 2008 just got shredded. Twenty more to go."*

**Body.** What the moment means. The arc it sits inside.

**Best on.** Instagram (Reel or photo), Facebook, YouTube Community.

### 10. The Process Post

**Hook.** Shows how the work happens.

> *"This morning's coaching call started with a question I wasn't expecting."*
> *"Here's the framework I run every operating decision through."*

**Body.** Walk through the actual process or moment.

**Close.** Soft direct or reflection.

**Best on.** LinkedIn, Instagram carousel, YouTube Community.

### 11. The Recommendation

**Hook.** A book, tool, framework, or person worth knowing about.

**Body.** Why it matters. How John uses it. Who else would benefit.

**Best on.** LinkedIn, Facebook, X.

### 12. The Anniversary or Milestone

**Hook.** A specific milestone in the operating arc.

> *"23 years ago this month, I signed papers on store number one."*
> *"Five years ago today, I made the snap decision that almost cost me everything."*

**Body.** The arc from then to now. The lesson that crystallized.

**Best on.** Every platform. Anchor moments earn cross-platform treatment.

### 13. The Community Celebration

**Hook.** Lifting up another operator, coach, or peer.

**Body.** What they did. Why it matters. Direct credit.

**Best on.** LinkedIn primarily. Instagram for visual celebrations.

### 14. The Quote Post

**Hook AND post.** A John signature phrase as a graphic.

> *"Letting go isn't quitting. It's completing."*
> *"Focus on the doing."*
> *"Not every store is meant to be saved."*

**Caption.** Optional 1 to 3 sentence context.

**Best on.** Instagram (graphic post), LinkedIn (image with caption), Facebook.

### Use Rules for Post Archetypes

- **Mix the registers.** A feed of only Back Office Confession posts exhausts the audience. A feed of only Quick Observations feels shallow. Both registers earn their place.
- **Match archetype to platform.** Long reflective posts win on LinkedIn and Facebook. Short hooks win on TikTok and X. Visual carousels win on Instagram and LinkedIn.
- **Recurring features build over time.** A "23 Years, 23 Lessons" series. A weekly "Operator Question." A Saturday-morning reflection. Recurring slots train the audience to expect and engage.

## Hook Craft for Social

Different platforms, different hook windows. Same underlying patterns.

### The Hook Patterns (carry across platforms)

**A. Confession.** *"I'm going to tell you about the hire I should have fired six months earlier."*

**B. Time anchor.** *"It was 2009. Saturday morning rush. My Lenoir City store."*

**C. Contrarian claim.** *"Most operators think more hours fix a struggling store. I tried that for two years."*

**D. Credibility specific.** *"After 23 years and 11 stores..."*

**E. Direct question.** *"Are you holding on out of purpose, or out of fear?"*

**F. Operator scene.** *"If you've ever sat in your store after close, lights off, wondering if it's still worth it..."*

**G. Quick truth.** *"You can't out-coach a hire who shouldn't be there."*

### Hook Rules

- LinkedIn and Facebook: hook in first three lines.
- Instagram caption: hook in first line.
- TikTok and Reels: hook in first 1.5 seconds.
- X: the whole post or the first post in the thread.
- One hook per post. Don't open with two.
- Visual and verbal hook reinforce on video formats.

## Carousel Craft

Carousels are their own discipline. LinkedIn and Instagram carousels are some of the highest-leverage content formats in the playbook.

### Structure

- **Cover slide.** Earns the swipe. Big text. Clear hook. *"3 lessons from 23 years of operating UPS Stores"* or *"The false belief that cost me two years."*
- **Middle slides.** One beat per slide. Don't try to land two ideas on one card.
- **Visual consistency.** Same fonts, same colors, same template across the carousel.
- **Last slide.** CTA or reflection. *"Save this for the next time you're stuck."* Or *"Take the scorecard at timefreedomscore.com."*

### Format Specs

- **LinkedIn.** 8 to 10 slides typical. PDF upload. Square or portrait.
- **Instagram.** Up to 10 slides. Square (1:1) or portrait (4:5).

### Content Patterns

- **The numbered list.** *"5 false beliefs operators carry."*
- **The framework breakdown.** Each slide for one Basic of the Four Basics.
- **The story walk.** Each slide one beat of a story arc.
- **The decision autopsy.** Slide 1 the decision, slide 2 the context, slide 3 the outcome, slide 4 the lesson.

### Carousel Rules

- One idea per slide. No exceptions.
- Cover slide earns the swipe. Without it, nothing else gets seen.
- Visual consistency across slides.
- Closing slide always either reflects or directs.

### Visual Treatment

- Brand-consistent colors and fonts.
- Attribution clean. *"John Nichols, XpansionCode."*

### Caption Strategy

- 1 to 3 sentence context that earned the quote.

- Soft direct or reflection at the close.

### Use Rule

Quote posts work in moderation. Once a week max. Saturate them and they lose force.

## Engagement Strategy

Social isn't broadcast. The comments are half the value.

### Replying to Comments

- Within first 2 hours when possible. Algorithm rewards early engagement.
- Reply to every comment for the first 24 hours on a post.
- Use replies to extend the post. Share an additional story, a follow-up thought, a related framework.
- Don't argue. Redirect. *"That's a fair take. I'd offer one other angle..."*

### Engaging on Other Operators' Posts

- Comment thoughtfully on operator and franchise content. Not "great post" comments. Real responses.
- Share wins from the community when they happen.
- Tag fellow operators in posts where their experience is relevant (with permission).

### Lifting the Community

- Celebrate other operators' milestones publicly.
- Quote and credit when you build on someone else's idea.
- Reply to questions in franchise owner groups even when there's no direct conversion path.

### Engagement Rules

- The peer-to-peer stance lives in the comments more than in the posts.
- Don't pitch in comments. Soft direct only.
- Don't ghost your own threads. If a comment is worth a reply, reply.

## Recurring Features and Series

Recurring slots train the audience to expect and engage.

### Examples

- **"23 Years, 23 Lessons" series.** One lesson per week for 23 weeks. Builds an arc.
- **"Saturday Morning Reflection" weekly.** A short reflective post every Saturday.
- **"Operator Question of the Week."** Asks the audience a question every Tuesday.
- **"Counter Stories" monthly.** Real moments from running stores.
- **"Decision Autopsy" monthly.** A walked-through decision, good or bad.
- **"Three Things I'm Learning Right Now" monthly.** Current reading, current insight, current challenge.

### Series Rules

- Pick 1 to 3 recurring features. Don't try to run six.
- Branded visual consistency for each series.
- Sustainable cadence. Weekly is the sweet spot. Daily burns out.
- Hashtag for each series so the archive is searchable.

## Hashtag and Mention Strategy

### Hashtags by Platform

- **LinkedIn.** 3 to 5 hashtags. Broad professional (#franchise #leadership #operator #franchisee).
- **Instagram.** 10 to 15 hashtags. Mix of broad (#franchise) and niche (#multiunitfranchise #upsstoreowner).
- **TikTok.** 3 to 5 hashtags. Trend-aware. Mix of broad and niche.
- **Facebook.** 0 to 3 hashtags. Hashtags don't drive much on Facebook.
- **X.** 1 to 2 hashtags max. Often zero.
- **Threads.** 0 to 2 hashtags.

### Mentioning Conventions

- Tag people only when you've genuinely engaged with their work or spoken with them.
- Tag fellow operators when sharing a story about them, with permission.
- Avoid tagging brands unsolicited as engagement bait.
- Credit sources cleanly. *"Hat tip to [name]"* or *"Inspired by [name]'s framework."*

## Captions and Accessibility

Same logic as video and ads. Captions are not optional.

### Captions on Video

- **TikTok and Reels.** Burn-in essential.
- **Instagram feed video.** Burn-in or platform captions.
- **LinkedIn native video.** Captions via SRT or burn-in.
- **Facebook video.** Captions via SRT or burn-in.

- Image alt text on every static post.
- Descriptive text in carousel slides for screen readers.

### Caption Style

- Match speech (don't sanitize).
- Keep texture (so, now, here's the thing).
- Sentence case.

## Self-Awareness Patterns on Social

Vulnerability lands hardest on social because the audience comments back. Used deliberately, it's the engagement engine.

### The 50/50 Snap-Decision Pattern

Lands a smile and trust at once. Highest-engagement single line in the playbook. Pull it out for any post about decision-making.

### The Bad Hire Confession

Universal. Models accountability. Comments will fill with operators sharing their own version.

### The Mary Ellen Truth

Models marriage and the wisdom mirror. Personal but operator-relevant.

### The Persistence-vs-Purpose Confession

Reframes a quality the audience has been told their whole career to value. Lands hard.

### The Back-Office Moment

Sensory specificity. Lights off, glow of the screen. Audiences feel the room.

### Use Rule

One vulnerability beat per post. Stacked vulnerability reads as performance. Spaced vulnerability reads as honesty.

## Faith on Social

Post-by-post calibration. Most posts stay implicit. Some go explicit.

### When to Go Explicit

- Sunday morning posts (cultural permission).
- Easter, Christmas, key faith holidays.
- Posts directly about prayer, scripture, or devotional practice.
- Posts on faith-aligned platforms or in faith-aligned groups.
- John Maxwell-related content (faith-forward audience).

### When to Stay Implicit

- Most LinkedIn posts (broad professional audience).
- Most Instagram and TikTok content (younger skew, broad).
- Industry commentary.
- Sales-adjacent posts.

### The Safe Middle Move

A single brief unforced reference. *"I started the day in the same chair I always do, with the same book."* Or *"This came up in my devotional this morning."* Audience members who share the faith feel seen. Others read it as a personal practice mention and move on.

### Rule of Thumb

When in doubt, implicit. The values carry. Reserve explicit for the contexts where it adds rather than narrows.

## Content Cadence and Mix

The right mix matters more than the right frequency.

### Suggested Weekly Mix (across platforms)

- **2 to 3 reflective long-form posts** (Back Office Confession, Decision Autopsy, Mary Ellen Mirror, Completion arc).
- **3 to 5 quick observations** (single-sentence truths, quote posts).
- **2 to 3 short-form videos** (Reels, TikTok, Shorts repurposed).
- **1 carousel** (False Belief Destroyed, framework breakdown, lesson series).
- **1 question post or community engagement** (Operator Question of the Week, celebration of someone else's work).
- **1 behind-the-scenes** (selling stores, current writing, current life).

### Cadence Rules

- Mix beats volume.
- Sustainable cadence beats burst-and-burnout.
- Take a week off when needed. Audience trust survives gaps if the voice stays consistent when you return.

## Coaching Patterns Carried to Social

Several patterns translate. Most adapt.

**Story-to-Lesson Pattern.** Carries strongly. Compressed for the platform.

**Self-Awareness-First Pattern.** Carries strongly. Social is where the 50/50 confession lands hardest because comments fill with peers sharing their own version.

**Reframe Pattern.** Carries. The reframe phrase is the post's central line.

**Diagnostic-Before-Advice Pattern.** Adjusts. Replaced with rhetorical questions and direct audience prompts.

**Action-Closer Pattern.** Adjusts. Soft on social. Reflection or save-worthy line over hard CTA.

**False Belief Pattern.** Carries. Workhorse for carousels and reflective posts.

**Community Pattern.** New on social. Lifting up other operators, celebrating peers, engaging on others' work. Doesn't fit any other medium.

## Writing Notes (for Social Posts)

* **No dashes.** Hard rule. Em dashes and en dashes get replaced with periods, commas, semicolons, colons, or parentheses. The original AI doc explicitly recommended dashes for emphasis. Strike that rule everywhere.
* **Real names, places, years.** *"My Lenoir City store in 2009"* beats *"one of my locations."*
* **No leadership-book language.** Cut *"synergy," "alignment," "buy-in," "leverage"* as a verb, *"circle back," "double down."*
* **Short paragraphs.** 1 to 3 sentences. White space matters as much on social as in email.
* **Read it out loud.** If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.
* **Save the reframe.** One per post. Pulled out as a standalone line with line breaks before and after for emphasis.
* **Repurpose deliberately.** A long LinkedIn post becomes three quick X posts becomes a Reel script becomes a quote post graphic. One source, many outputs.
